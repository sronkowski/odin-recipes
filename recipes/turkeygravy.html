Turkey Gravy

Description

Gravy is the ultimate accompaniment to turkey, but making good gravy is more than just opening a packet and praying for succcess.

The key to any good sauce is a good base, and since our goal for a good gravy is to enhance the natural flavor of turkey, we will start by making a double stock using turkey parts. With this stock made, all that's left is to make a quick roux and reduce to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 5+ lb assorted turkey parts - you can find smoked turkey necks and tails and many grocery stores
  • 4 onions, finely diced
  • 4 stalks celery, finely diced
  • 6 carrots, finely diced
  • Fresh sage
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Take half of the vegetables, and saute until well-browned.
  2. Add in half of the turkey parts, and saute them until well-browned
  3. Cover all ingredients with enough water to submerge them, and then cook at 15PSI in a pressure cooker for 40 minutes
  4. Let pressure release naturally, and strain stock through a collander to remove turkey parts and vegetables
  5. Repeat steps 1-3, but use stock from first round instead of water to cover ingredients - this is called a "double stock"
  6. Pressure cook again at 15PSI for 40 minutes
  7. Strain ingredients, and then filter stock through a paper towel to remove any remaining small bits of food
  8. Make a roux using 4 tbsp flour and 4 tbsp butter
  9. Once roux is made, add a small amount of stock and stir rapidly over medium heat until well-combined
  10. Continue to add stock to roux mixture, waiting for gravy to thicken before adding more
  11. Simmer over low heat, skimming scum off of gravy as needed
  12. Before serving, add salt and pepper to taste
